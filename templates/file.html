About Us

Agriculture is an important sector in Nepal's economy, employing over half of the country's workforce and contributing significantly to its GDP. Nepal has a long history of agriculture, with the earliest known evidence of cultivation dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization around 6000 BCE. The country is known for its diverse range of crops, including rice, wheat, sugarcane, cotton, and tea, as well as various fruits and vegetables.

Agriculture also plays an important role in Nepal's cultural and social life, with many festivals and traditions centered around the planting and harvesting of crops. Overall, agriculture is a vital component of Nepal's economy and society, and will continue to be so for the foreseeable future.
